About Me

Biography

Dual-major PhD candidate in Computational Linguistics and Middle Eastern Languages & Cultures at Indiana University Bloomington. My research focuses on Arabic Natural Language Processing with applications in dialect identification, word sense disambiguation, and computational semantics.

With a background in English Linguistics and extensive teaching experience, I bridge the gap between theoretical linguistics and practical computational applications, particularly for Arabic language technologies.

Research Interests

Arabic NLP

Developing tools and models specifically for Arabic language processing, including dialect identification and morphological analysis.

Collocations

Researching Arabic collocations for language instruction and automated assessment systems.

Word Sense Disambiguation

Developing methods to automatically determine the correct meaning of words in context for Arabic.

Dialect Identification

Creating models to identify and classify Arabic dialects in text, particularly from social media.

Computational Semantics

Applying computational methods to understand meaning in Arabic texts, including semantic role labeling.

Knowledge Graphs

Building structured knowledge representations for Arabic domains, including finance and medicine.

Current Projects

Detecting ellipsis with large language models

Developing methods to identify and reconstruct elided words in Arabic texts using state-of-the-art language models.

NLP Arabic LLMs

Arabic financial knowledge graph

Constructing a comprehensive knowledge graph for Arabic financial domains to support information extraction and question answering.
